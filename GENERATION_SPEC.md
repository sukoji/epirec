# EpiRec — synthetic episodic-recall benchmark: generation specification

**Status: pre-registered.** This document was written and frozen BEFORE any
episode or probe was generated, and before any retrieval strategy was run on
the data. The generation rules below do not reference, and were not informed
by, the ranking behaviour of any retrieval method.

## Purpose

Evaluate episodic-memory retrieval for journaling agents along the axis no
public benchmark covers: **emotional resurfacing** — recalling an emotionally
significant past episode from a reflective, lexically-indirect prompt — while
also measuring factual lookup on the same store, so that any emotional gain
that costs factual recall is visible.

## Provenance & ethics

- 100 % synthetic. Every persona, event, and utterance is fictional; no real
  person, place-of-employment detail, or user data. Generated by Claude
  (Anthropic), authored file-by-file in a supervised session.
- Disclosed conflict: generated by the same session that maintains the
  Persode reference implementation. Mitigations: this frozen spec, the
  independence rules below, full publication of spec + data, and a
  human-validated label sample (see Label protocol).
- Content constraints: everyday-life emotional range (loss of a pet,
  arguments, exam stress, celebrations). No self-harm, abuse, medical or
  crisis content. All names are common given names with no surnames.
- Intended license on release: CC BY 4.0.

## Independence rules (anti-circularity)

1. **No lexicon awareness.** The generator must not consult
   `persode/analyzer.py`'s keyword lexicon while writing episodes or probes.
   Episodes are written for a *target intensity band* (below), in natural
   language; whether the offline analyzer detects them is a *measured result*,
   never a construction input.
2. **No method awareness.** Rules never condition on what similarity, recency,
   salience, or fused rankings would return.
3. **Symmetry.** Every episode — emotional or neutral — gets the same probe
   types under the same construction rules. No episode is probed more or less
   because a method is expected to do well on it.

## Corpus shape

- 12 personas, each an independent journaling "user" with a name, an age in
  the 16–35 range, an occupation/context, and 2–3 recurring life threads.
- Per persona: 14–18 episodes across 10–14 sessions spanning ~120 days of
  fictional time. Session timestamps are explicit datetimes; the evaluation
  reference `now` is the corpus anchor **2025-06-30 12:00 UTC**, ≥ 1 day after
  every persona's last session.
- Episode mix per persona (counts fixed per persona file, targets in
  aggregate): ~45 % neutral routine (target intensity band E ≤ 0.3), ~40 %
  emotionally significant (band E ≥ 0.7; balanced across positive and negative
  valence), ~15 % mild (0.4–0.6). Ages must spread so that both the ≤ 6-day
  short-term window and the long tail (> 30 days) are populated for both
  emotional and neutral episodes.
- Episode text: 1–3 sentences of first-person journaling voice, self-contained
  (understandable without other episodes), 15–60 words.

## Labels

Each episode carries, assigned at authoring time as part of its design (not
derived from any analyzer):

- `intensity_band`: `high` (≥ 0.7) | `mild` (0.4–0.6) | `low` (≤ 0.3)
- `valence`: `positive` | `negative` | `neutral`
- `emotion`: free-form dominant emotion word (e.g., grief, joy, anxiety)

**Label protocol.** Bands are gold *by construction* (the episode was written
to sit in that band). Validation: a stratified random sample of ≥ 60 episodes
is independently rated by a human (band + valence); agreement is reported in
the dataset card. Disagreements are reported, not silently fixed; episodes
whose human rating contradicts the band are flagged `label_disputed: true`
and excluded from label-dependent metrics.

## Probes

Every episode gets exactly 3 probes, one of each type:

1. `factual` — a direct question about a concrete detail of the episode
   ("What did I order at the new ramen place?"). May reuse content words.
2. `reflective_explicit` — first-person reflective prompt that names the
   emotion or an obvious synonym ("I still feel so proud when I think about
   finishing that race").
3. `reflective_implicit` — first-person reflective prompt that conveys the
   feeling **without any emotion keyword and without reusing content words**
   from the episode text ("That evening by the water still comes back to me
   sometimes"). Rule of thumb: no token with the same stem as any
   content word of the episode, no words from a standard emotion vocabulary.

Probe target = its episode (single-target). Each probe is answerable from the
persona's store alone; probes must not be ambiguous between two episodes of
the same persona (checked at authoring time; ambiguous pairs get the probe
rewritten before freeze).

## Evaluation protocol (fixed here)

Same as Persode Exp. 3/5: per persona, all episodes form one store; strategies
recency-only / similarity-only / salience-only / fused (α = 0.5) /
gated (agent rule) rank the store per probe via `MemoryStore.retrieve` at
shipped defaults; metrics recall@3 (primary), recall@1, MRR; reported overall,
per probe type, and per (probe type × episode band × short/long-term);
both hashing and sentence-transformers embedders. Stored C = 0.5,
recall_count = 0. E for *scoring* comes from the system's analyzer (as in the
live agent); the authored bands serve as ground truth for stratification —
this separation is the point: it measures the whole pipeline, analyzer
included, against labels it never saw.

## Freeze discipline

After the corpus is frozen (git commit + SHA-256 in the eval output), episodes
and probes are never edited in response to results. Errors found later are
handled by versioning the dataset (v1.1) with a changelog, never by silent
in-place edits.
